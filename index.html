Problem Statement

Magnetic Reconnection

The problem statement is about predicting magnetic reconnection events and forecasting, to take preventive measures to escape the adverse impacts observed on Earth's surface and the satellites orbiting Earth. Our objective is to develop a computer program for the public that performs statistical and spectral analysis of the vector components of the IMF measured by the ACE, WIND, and DSCOVR missions to assess how often magnetic reconnection occurs. Our analysis will provide vital information regarding how frequently solar wind particles and energy gain access into the geo-space environment and potentially cause space weather effects.

The Project

☀ To predict the space weather conditions near Earth, it is necessary to provide solar wind conditions upstream of Earth in advance. Currently, spacecraft at the Sun–Earth L1 point continuously monitor the incoming solar wind 1.4 million km ahead of Earth.​

☀ However, the typical travel time for the solar wind from the L1 point to the outer edges of the magnetosphere is only one hour at most, which is too short to provide a warning of adverse space weather events. These events can disrupt electricity on Earth's surface and negatively impact Satellites orbiting Earth​.

☀ We have developed an LSTM model and trained it on OMNI dataset with features such as plasma density, solar wind speed, Bz, plasma temperature and DST values. Our model is capable is predicting MR events accurately well in advance and provide us the required temporal window to defend ourselves from adverse space events.​

Use of AI

☀ Artificial intelligence is used to detect and predict magnetic reconnection measured by the ACE, WIND, and DSCOVR missions via an LSTM model.

☀ LSTM models learn long-term dependencies in data, which makes them ideal for analyzing time series data, such as the vector components of the IMF measured by the ACE, WIND, and DSCOVR missions.

☀ The LSTM model gives prediction of the current time stamp and the next hour time stamp upon training on the various IMF and plasma features. It has an accuracy of 89.13 and is able to detect magnetic reconnection in the dataset significantly well.
